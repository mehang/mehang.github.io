Testing of the magnifier

Since the video recording did not capture the magnified view, I had to write down my experience. A magnifying tool is just a simple software that increase the size of the content, similar to magnifying glass. Windows Operating System provides a very good magnifier tool. It comes pre-installed within the operating system. It was interesting that along with different scale of magnifying it gave different mode of magnification:
  • Full screen - Magnifies the content and displays it on the whole screen
  • Lens - Magnifies the content surrounding the cursor and displays only on the rectangular display that follows the cursor
  • Docked - Magnifies the portion surrounding the cursor and display on a separate display docked at the top of the screen
I used different magnification scale and its mode for reading a blog of soccer news on https://goal.com. I used it for quite sometime, and since I am used to looking at normal mode i.e. 100%, my eye was strained at higher zoom level because of the magnified texts and icons. I also had to move around the cursor a lot because only a small portion of content could be contained in the magnified view. Also, since I didn't have a mouse I also used to continuously press the arrow keys to navigate webpage sometimes, as the touchpad did not allowed to do so. A 300% zoom was too much for me as I did not get enough context in that view, but I would say for low vision people it might be useful although a bit time consuming. The higher the zoom level, the more scrolling there is. Out of the three modes, I liked the docked mode because it gives the user a point of reference in regular webpage through cursor and separates the zoomed view on the top dock of the screen, which is wider than the lens mode. The lens mode is good but I lost track of content when switching in between lines as the magnified view is overlayed on top of the cursor. In full screen mode, I did not seem to have a point of reference as a whole and many things at higher zoom level were clipped away unlike in other two mode where the non-magnified section are still visible. But at the end, I would like to say that this magnifying tool is really good and I think it will definitely help low vision people, but I have concern regarding how it will be helpful in viewing video will be helpful because a lot of content will either be non-magnified or clipped out. I hope that will be addressed in the future. The picture from my testing are present over here. https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/13BbIE4bGKzgbdIP33KwVeKT5DPi_bSh0?usp=sharing.

Testing of the high-contrast

Microsoft windows also provides toggling of high contrast. I test it for reading the blog and I really liked it. All the white colors were inverted to black, so it really relaxed my eyes and also all the contents were quite distinguishable. For someone with color blindness or low vision, this could be really helpful. The contrasting color could save time in finding the content or web element. The picture from my testing are present over here.https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/13BbIE4bGKzgbdIP33KwVeKT5DPi_bSh0?usp=sharing.

Features added to this site for low vision users

To assist the users with low vision, zooming features was added to the application. There are two buttons at the top of the website for increasing and decreasing the font size. The magnification is not limited to certain choice. As long as the button is clicked the content will keep growing or shrinking in size. Right now, limit has not been set as it is difficult to assume what is the right limit for low vision users because they can have varying degree of visions. Along with zooming feature, high-contrast feature was added too. It helps to easily distinguish/differentiate the background color with the color of contents in the front. So, any item or content can be pointed to immediately. A high-contrast can also aid some of the color blind people because high-contrast mode could turn into something more perceivable not only for the low vision users but for the color blind people as well. Since these two functions could be combined, users could combine and use them according to their preference.
